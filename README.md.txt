# E-Commerce SQL Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes an e-commerce dataset using SQL to understand customer purchasing behavior, revenue trends, and operational performance.

The analysis simulates real-world business questions that a data analyst might investigate to support business decision-making.

---

## Tools Used

- PostgreSQL
- SQL
- Google Sheets

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains information about an e-commerce platform including:

- Customers
- Orders
- Order items
- Payments
- Reviews
- Products

Approximate dataset size:

- ~99,000 orders
- ~99,000 customers
- ~112,000 order items
- ~103,000 payments
- ~32,000 products

---

## Business Questions Answered

1. What is the total revenue generated by the platform?
2. How does revenue change month-to-month?
3. Which products generate the most revenue?
4. Who are the highest-value customers?
5. Do late deliveries impact customer reviews?
6. Which regions generate the most revenue?
7. What is the average revenue per customer?

---

## Key Insights

- Revenue increased as the platform expanded.
- A small number of product categories generate a large portion of revenue.
- High-value customers contribute significantly to total sales.
- Late deliveries tend to receive lower review scores.
- Revenue varies significantly by geographic region.
